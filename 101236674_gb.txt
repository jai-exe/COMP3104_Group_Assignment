The college was established during the formation of Ontario's community college system in 1967. 
Colleges of Applied Arts and Technology were established on May 21, 1965. 
The college is named after George Brown, who was an important 19th century politician and newspaper publisher (he founded the Toronto Globe, forerunner to The Globe and Mail) and was one of the Fathers of Confederation.

In 2014, George Brown College has been ranked the third-best research college in the country, climbing 19 spots in the rankings from the previous year. 
Research Infosource, which publishes annual rankings reports on research and development at institutions across Canada, released the results for the top 50 colleges on Oct. 22, 2014.
George Brown led 16 Ontario colleges "by attracting $9.3 million of research funding" in the 2013 fiscal year.
